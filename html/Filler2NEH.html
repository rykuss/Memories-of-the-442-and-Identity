Memories of the 442 and Identity

In the months following the attack on Pearl Harbor, you try to continue life as normal. However, the treatment of Japanese-Americans on Hawaii is markedly different than before. Blackouts and a nightly curfew affect everyone, but Japanese citizens are specially marked on identification cards. The derogatory term ‘Jap’ is now commonly used to refer to Japanese. Nevertheless many Japanese Hawaiians desire to enlist under the constant threat of another Japanese attack on the islands.
