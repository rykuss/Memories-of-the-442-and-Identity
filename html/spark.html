Memories of the 442 and Identity

You have most identified with Spark Matsunaga. Spark Matsunaga was born in Kauai, and became one of the first Japanese Americans to serve in the Senate after serving in the House of Representatives. He was a First Lieutenant, and was wounded twice during his time in the 442nd team and the 100th battalion. After the war, he focused on law, and when he became a congressman, he helped introduce a bill that abolished the Emergency Detention Act, so that relocation camps could never be set up again. He was one of the few Japanese American politicians that arose in Hawaii, along with Daniel Inouye, never straying from his ideals that Japanese Americans should be treated the same as any other American
