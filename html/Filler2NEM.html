Memories of the 442 and Identity

Moved from your home to the Heart Mountain Relocation Camp, you and your family live in poorly constructed (but electrically-powered) barracks with 10,000 others. Japanese-Americans provide most of the labor required to keep the camp going, and are not allowed to earn more than a private in the army - about 12-19 dollars per month. In 1943, the first all-Japanese regiment is formed and following the low number of volunteers, in part due to the confusing loyalty questionnaire, the draft is extended to Nisei.
