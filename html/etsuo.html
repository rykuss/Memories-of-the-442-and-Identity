Memories of Japanese American Identity

You have most identified with Etsuo Kohashi. The son of a fisherman and born in the city of Hilo, Hawaii, he was known as “Etchan” to members of the 442nd Infantry Regiment. A sergeant in Company K, he was known for carrying out daring reconnaissance work. Terrified of becoming a prisoner of war, he was especially afraid of the possibility of being sent to Japan and subsequently executed to the shame of his relatives (certainly an unlikely probability). His uncle - a Russo-Japanese War veteran - told him to “come back dead” in order to assuage his worries about home and to better focus on combat.

Duus, Masayo. Unlikely Liberators: The Men of The 100th and 442nd. University of Hawaii Press, 2006.
